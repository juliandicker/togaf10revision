# TOGAF® 10 – Part 2 Reasoning Framework  
*A structured method for consistently selecting the “most correct” answer*

The TOGAF Part 2 exam is not about memorisation — it is about **reasoning**.  
Every question has:
- 1 *best* answer (5 points)  
- 1 *good* answer (3 points)  
- 1 *weak but plausible* answer (1 point)  
- 1 *incorrect* answer (0 points)

This framework helps you reliably identify the 5‑point answer.

---

# 🎯 1. Start With the Scenario Objective
Ask yourself:
- *What is the architect trying to achieve right now?*
- *What is the business driver or pain point?*
- *What phase of the ADM does this align with?*

The correct answer always aligns with the **primary scenario objective**, not side details.

---

# 🎯 2. Identify the ADM Phase
Most questions implicitly test:
- “Which ADM phase is this?”
- “What step in the phase should occur next?”

Use the ADM to anchor your reasoning:
- Vision → clarify scope, stakeholders, constraints  
- B/C/D → develop architectures, perform gap analysis  
- E → consolidate into work packages  
- F → prioritise and plan migrations  
- G → govern implementation  
- H → manage change  

If an answer jumps ahead or repeats a previous phase, it’s wrong.

---

# 🎯 3. Look for Governance vs. Delivery
TOGAF separates:
- **Architecture work** (ADM)  
- **Solution delivery** (projects, implementation teams)

If an option describes:
- building solutions  
- coding  
- configuring systems  
- running projects  

…it is almost always **incorrect**.

---

# 🎯 4. Prioritise Stakeholder Alignment
The best answer often:
- engages stakeholders  
- resolves conflicts  
- clarifies concerns  
- aligns expectations  
- communicates architecture intent  

TOGAF is stakeholder‑driven.  
If an option ignores stakeholders, it’s rarely correct.

---

# 🎯 5. Follow the “TOGAF Tone”
Correct answers tend to be:
- methodical  
- governance‑oriented  
- collaborative  
- documentation‑driven  
- iterative  
- aligned with the ADM  

Incorrect answers tend to be:
- reactive  
- tactical  
- solution‑driven  
- skipping steps  
- overly technical  

---

# 🎯 6. Use the “Elimination Ladder”
Eliminate answers in this order:

1. **Incorrect** (0‑point)  
   - contradicts TOGAF  
   - jumps phases  
   - describes solution delivery  
   - ignores stakeholders  

2. **Weak** (1‑point)  
   - technically possible but not ideal  
   - incomplete  
   - too narrow  

3. **Good** (3‑point)  
   - reasonable  
   - aligns with ADM  
   - but not the *best* strategic move  

4. **Best** (5‑point)  
   - aligns with scenario objective  
   - aligns with ADM phase  
   - addresses stakeholders  
   - moves architecture work forward  

---

# 🎯 7. When in Doubt, Choose the Option That:
- Clarifies scope  
- Engages stakeholders  
- Uses an ADM step  
- Improves governance  
- Documents decisions  
- Reduces risk  
- Prepares for the next phase  

These are the hallmarks of TOGAF‑correct reasoning.

---

# 📌 Summary
This framework helps you:
- Anchor your reasoning in the ADM  
- Avoid delivery‑focused distractors  
- Identify governance‑aligned answers  
- Select the 5‑point option consistently  

Use this together with the **12 Scenario Patterns Cheat‑Sheet** for maximum effect.
