# TOGAF® 10 – Part 2 Exam Navigation Guide  
*A practical reference for mastering the open‑book exam environment*

This guide lists the **exact sections of the TOGAF Standard, 10th Edition** you should practise navigating for the Part 2 (Practitioner) exam.  
These are the sections that directly support scenario‑based reasoning and appear repeatedly in official practice questions.

---

## ⭐ 1. Core ADM Sections (Highest Priority)

### ADM Overview
- **Chapter 5 – Introduction to the ADM**
- **Chapter 6 – The ADM Phases**
- **Chapter 7 – Adapting the ADM**
- **Chapter 8 – Applying the ADM**

### Individual ADM Phases (Chapters 9–17)
- **Preliminary Phase**
- **Phase A – Architecture Vision**
- **Phase B – Business Architecture**
- **Phase C – Information Systems Architectures**
- **Phase D – Technology Architecture**
- **Phase E – Opportunities & Solutions**
- **Phase F – Migration Planning**
- **Phase G – Implementation Governance**
- **Phase H – Architecture Change Management**

> **Why this matters:**  
> Part 2 questions frequently ask what the architect should do next, which phase applies, or which step is appropriate.  
> Fast navigation here is essential.

# 🎯 Recommended Navigation Drills

### Drill 1 — ADM Jumping
Pick a random ADM phase and navigate to:
- Objectives  
- Inputs  
- Steps  
- Outputs  

**Target:** 3–5 seconds.

### Drill 2 — Concept Location
Pick a concept (e.g., “Stakeholder Management”) and locate:
- Its main chapter  
- Where it appears in ADM phases  

**Target:** 5–7 seconds.

### Drill 3 — Deliverable Identification
Pick a deliverable (e.g., Architecture Definition Document) and find:
- Its definition in Chapter 23  
- Where it is produced in the ADM  

**Target:** 5–10 seconds.
